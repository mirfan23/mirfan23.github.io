02 — About

Why cooperative fintech, specifically

Most of what I build sits at an unglamorous intersection: people who need to move money, get paid, clock in, or manage stock — reliably, every day, without thinking about the app underneath it. Coordinator at a cooperative checking today's PPOB transactions. A field technician logging a generator reading with a geotagged photo. A merchant moving a balance to their bank account before close.

That's the work I keep returning to: modular Flutter architecture that can carry marketplace, payments, attendance, and logistics in a single app without collapsing under its own feature list. REST APIs and Firebase wired up so the business logic — not the UI — gets to be the hard part.

I work mainly in Flutter and Kotlin, and I'm most interested in the apps that don't get to fail quietly — the ones handling someone's balance, payroll, or attendance record on a given day.
